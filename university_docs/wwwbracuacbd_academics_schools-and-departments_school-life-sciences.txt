School of Life Sciences (SLS)
The School of Life Sciences at BRAC University is a premier hub for biological inquiry and technological innovation, dedicated to exploring the complexities of life from the molecular level to entire ecosystems. The school bridges fundamental scientific understanding with practical applications to address critical global challenges in health, agriculture, food security, and environmental sustainability.
Comprising the
Department of Microbiology,
Department of Biotechnology
, and the Department of Agriculture Technology, the School offers a multidisciplinary environment where biology meets technology. With a rigorous academic foundation and a strong emphasis on research, the school prepares students to become leaders in the rapidly evolving life sciences landscape, fostering a culture of discovery that links science with real-world solutions.
Assistant Dean's Message
To be a distinguished hub of interdisciplinary excellence where the convergence of biological sciences and technology drives transformative solutions. We aspire to lead the Global South region in bio-innovation, fostering a research-driven ecosystem that addresses the critical nexus of human health, sustainable agriculture, and environmental stewardship.
Academic Excellence: To offer comprehensive, outcome-based curricula that equip students with profound theoretical knowledge and advanced hands-on skills. We aim to produce graduates who are adaptable, technically competent, and ready for careers in research, industry, and public service.
Research & Innovation: To foster a vibrant research culture that encourages interdisciplinary collaboration and global connectivity. By investigating areas such as microbial dynamics, genomics, and sustainable agriculture, we aim to generate knowledge that directly contributes to the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
Societal Impact: To instill integrity and professional ethics in our graduates, empowering them to contribute to the betterment of society. We are committed to connecting science with society through public health initiatives, community awareness, and the development of sustainable industrial and agricultural practices.
