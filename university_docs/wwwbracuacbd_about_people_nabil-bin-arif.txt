Nabil Bin Arif is an academic, strategist, and advocate for purposeful innovation. His career, spanning Toronto, Cambridge, Jakarta, Guadalajara, San Francisco, Osaka, and Dhaka, reflects a commitment to leadership across business, education, governance, and social impact.
At BRAC University, he teaches business communications, business ethics, human resource management, and entrepreneurship, bridging theory with real-world application. He also chairs the board of directors at Motivate Canada, shaping programs that equip young leaders to drive meaningful change in education, athletics, and community development.
A recognized speaker and moderator, he served as master of ceremonies for the 9th Social Business Youth Summit 2025, hosted by YY Ventures and Yunus Centre, where he guided discussions on sustainable enterprise and impact-driven business. As a TEDx speaker at TEDxBRACU 2025, he shared insights on timeless leadership principles – grace, grit, gratitude, and goodness.
His governance experience includes elected and appointed roles at the University of Toronto Governing Council, Academic Board, Academic Appeals Committee, and Mississauga Campus Council. He was awarded the Gordon Cressy Leadership Award for his contributions to student leadership and institutional development. In addition to serving as a consultant for UNDP Indonesia, he has advised the Canadian Red Cross National Youth Committee.
Beyond academia, he has contributed to collegiate administration, advising, and recruitment at St. Michael’s College, University of Toronto, shaping academic pathways and guiding students through the complexities of higher education with a focus on long-term impact.
He has also played a key role in his family’s business, gaining firsthand experience in operations, strategy, and sustainable growth. This foundation in business, combined with his academic and governance experience, informs his pragmatic approach to leadership and enterprise.
As an executive committee member of the Canada Bangladesh Alumni Association (CBAA), he actively fosters cross-cultural collaboration, alumni engagement, and knowledge exchange.
Nabil remains dedicated to bridging tradition and modernity, shaping the future of business, governance, and education through thoughtful leadership and strategic vision.
