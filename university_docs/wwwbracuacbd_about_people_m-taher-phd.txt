Md. Abu Taher
(born in 1962) is a Professor at BRAC Business School, Brac University, Dhaka. He completed his B.Com (Hons.) and M.Com in Management at Chittagong University (CU), where he stood First Class First in both. Later, he pursued advanced studies at Inha University, South Korea, and Texas A&M University, USA. He earned his Ph.D. with a dissertation titled “
State Intervention in Industrial Relations
,” which explored the role of the state in shaping labour and industrial relations in colonial and post-colonial societies.
Professor Taher began his career in government service after qualifying in the General BCS Examination in 1985. However, within a short span, he switched to an academic career as a Lecturer on February 15, 1988, and later became Professor of Management at CU on May 10, 2004. Over his 38 years of teaching, he has had the privilege of holding several academic and administrative positions, including Chairman of the Department of Management, CU; elected Dean of the Faculty of Business Administration, CU; Treasurer of Bangladesh Open University; Pro-Vice Chancellor of Southeast University; Vice-Chancellor of CU; and Member of the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Bangladesh.
Internationally, he has served as a Non-tenure Track Foreign Professor at Inha University, South Korea, and as Visiting Professor at the Centre for Change and Conflict Resolution ,Texas and the Korean Academy of Business Ethics, Seoul. As a Fulbright Fellow, he was a Visiting Scholar in the Department of Educational Administration & Human Resource (EAHR) at Texas A&M University, USA.
An active scholar, Professor Taher has authored and co-authored 11 books at the undergraduate and postgraduate levels. He has to his credit over 100 research articles and conference papers published in refereed journals at home and abroad. His teaching portfolio includes courses in Industrial Relations, Human Resource Management (HRM), Human Resource Development (HRD), Strategic Management, Leadership, Organisational Behaviour, and Entrepreneurship. His research interests encompass HRM, HRD, organisational behaviour, entrepreneurship development, educational technology, industrial relations, and conflict management and resolution.
His scholarly work is widely published, with articles appearing in prestigious journals such as
the European Journal of Training and Development, Korean Journal of Business Ethics, Indian Journal of Open Learning, Creativity and Innovation, South Asian Journal of Management, SANGRAM International Journal of Multidisciplinary Research, British Journal of Industrial Relations, Indian Journal of Industrial Relations, HRD International, Journal of Business Administration, Global Journal of Management and Business Research
, and others.
Apart from teaching, he has completed several research projects funded by the World Bank, ILO, ADB, Commonwealth of Learning (COL), UGC, British Council, and other international organizations. His academic contributions continue to influence research, teaching, and policy in management and higher education both in Bangladesh and abroad.
