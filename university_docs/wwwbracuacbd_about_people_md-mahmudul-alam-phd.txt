Dr. Md. Mahmudul Alam
is a Professor of Finance at BRAC Business School, BRAC University. He also serves as a Senior Research Fellow at the Economic and Financial Policy Institute, Universiti Utara Malaysia (UUM), where he was a faculty member for over a decade. In addition, he holds Associate Fellow appointments at Universiti Teknologi MARA (Malaysia) and the Centre for Asian Climate and Environmental Policy Studies (Canada), and has served as a visiting professor at leading institutions in Indonesia, Iraq, Thailand, and Bangladesh.
He holds a PhD in Environment and Development Finance from the National University of Malaysia (UKM) and completed his postdoctoral fellowship through the Adaptation Finance Fellowship Programme (AFFP), jointly administered by the Frankfurt School of Finance & Management and the Thailand Development Research Institute. Dr. Alam is also a Registered Financial Planner with the Malaysian Financial Planning Council (MFPC) and a Certified Expert in Climate Adaptation Finance.
Dr. Alam has authored more than 300 publications, including seven books and over 200 peer-reviewed journal articles. His research appears in leading international journals such as
Technological Forecasting and Social Change, Journal of Retailing and Consumer Services, Sustainable Production and Consumption, Journal of Cleaner Production, Renewable Energy, Ecological Indicators, Sustainability Accounting Management and Policy Journal, Business Process Management Journal, Economic Modelling, Journal of Environmental Planning and Management, among others.
He serves on the editorial boards of 24 international journals and reviews for more than 50 academic outlets.
His work has been accessed by over two million readers globally. He has received over 11,000 citations with an h-index of 54 on Google Scholar, and 4,000+ citations with an h-index of 28 on Scopus. He has been recognized in the Top 2% of Scientists Worldwide by Stanford University, and is currently ranked as Bangladesh’s top researcher in Finance by the AD Scientific Index. His research excellence has also earned recognition from Elsevier, Emerald, Wiley, and Web of Science, and he was honored as a Top Bangladesh Development Researcher of the Millennium by the Bangladesh Development Research Center (BDRC), USA.
Dr. Alam’s research interests include financial literacy, financial markets, Islamic finance, sustainable finance, and sustainable development, with a strong emphasis on real-world impact and inclusive progress.
