Md. Shafiqul Islam

mdshafiqul.islam@bracu.ac.bd
Kha-224 Merul Badda
Dhaka 1212. Bangladesh
is a Senior Lecturer in Accounting at BRAC Business School, BRAC University, where he brings a strong blend of academic excellence and international research experience to his role. As a Fulbright Scholar of the US Department of State, he completed his Master of Science in Accounting and Analytics at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), USA, graduating with a perfect 4.0 GPA and earning induction into the Beta Gamma Sigma Honor Society.
He has previously taught as an adjunct faculty member at the Bangladesh Institute of Capital Markets (BICM) and in the MSc in Data Science and Analytics program at East West University. Prior to joining BRAC University, he served as a Senior Lecturer at East West University from 2019
to 2025, where he was tenured based on exemplary teaching and research performance. He was also the inaugural department‑level champion of the Three Minute Thesis (3MT) competition at the East West University Research Day 2025. Mr. Islam has practical experience of curriculum
development, ACBSP accreditation, and interdisciplinary teaching across accounting, analytics, and business education. He has consistently achieved high instructor ratings across multiple courses, including Introduction to Business (4.9/5), Financial Accounting (4.8/5), Accounting Information Systems (4.8/5), and Management Accounting (4.7/5). He has also designed innovative curriculum modules such as Accounting Data Analytics &amp; Visualization and Machine Learning for Accounting, and has taught specialized graduate‑level courses in Data Analytics for Finance (Rating: 4.8/5) and Machine Learning for Finance.
Before pursuing graduate studies in the United States, he distinguished himself academically in Bangladesh, earning two Gold Medals—one as the Valedictorian of the MBA in Accounting Information Systems program and another for achieving the highest CGPA among Bachelor level
cohorts at Kabi Jasim Uddin Hall, University of Dhaka.
Mr. Islam’s research centers on contemporary issues in
accounting
, with a strong emphasis on how emerging technologies are reshaping corporate reporting, audit quality, and information environments. His recent publications examine topics such as integrated reporting and value creation, earnings manipulation, intellectual capital disclosure, and the role of governance mechanisms in firm performance. He increasingly applies
computational methods,
including Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning, to analyze unstructured accounting narratives and assess the credibility of disclosures in areas such as sustainability reporting and corporate transparency. He is committed to advancing modern accounting education by integrating data‑driven approaches into the curriculum, particularly in areas related to corporate disclosure analytics, audit innovation, and the future of assurance

Graduate Level
Data Analytics for Finance
Objective: To apply computational techniques (using Python and Tableau) for cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing large financial datasets to uncover actionable insights.

Machine Learning for Finance
Objective: To implement predictive models for credit scoring, fraud detection, and stock price forecasting using algorithms like regression, decision trees, and neural networks.
Relevance: Critical for navigating the intersection of artificial intelligence and capital markets, bridging the gap between data science and financial theory.
Portfolio Management and Investment Analysis
Objective: To master asset allocation strategies, risk management frameworks, and valuation techniques for optimizing investment portfolios.
Relevance: Directly applicable to careers in investment banking, asset management, and financial analysis.

Financial Accounting
Objective: To understand the preparation and analysis of financial statements in accordance with IFRS/IAS and GAAP.
Relevance: The foundation of business language, essential for communicating economic performance to stakeholders.
Managerial Accounting
Objective: To utilize accounting information for internal decision-making, performance evaluation, budgeting, and strategic planning.
Relevance: Fundamental for future managers to learn how to optimize resources and drive organizational value.
