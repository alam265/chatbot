Dr. Najmul Hasan
is currently working as an
in Information Systems (IS) at BRAC Business School, BRAC University, Dhaka, Bangladesh. Dr. Hasan has been recognized as the
World’s Top 2% Scientist
in the IS domain (Stanford University/ Elsevier) in 2024 and 2025. He was a Former Visiting Academic at the School of Information and Physical Sciences (Data Science and Statistics), The Newcastle University, Australia. Dr. Hasan received his Ph.D. majoring in Management Science and Engineering with a specialization in Information Systems from the School of Management, Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) (AACSB and AMBA accredited), Wuhan, China. His research interests include information systems, predictive analytics, and machine learning. He has published 54 journal articles in numerous top-ranked international journals successively and 4 conference papers. Dr. Hasan’s major publications are in
Information Sciences, Information Systems, Telematics and Informatics, Internet of Things, Information Technology for Development, Enterprise Information Systems, Computers in Industry, Aslib Journal of Information Management, Education and Information Technologies, Journal of Retailing and Consumer Services, Children and Youth Services Review, Journal of Community Psychology, Energy,
and many more. He received the “Excellent Academic Award” at HUST for three consecutive academic years (2018–2019, 2019–2020, and 2020–2021). He was also awarded “HUST 2022 Honorary International Graduates” (for outstanding research performance). Dr. Hasan is serving as an
Academic Editor
for the journal PLoS One,
Assistant Editor
for Journal of Systems and Information Technology,
Editorial Board member
for Humanities and Social Sciences Communications,
Guest Editor
for Human-Centric Intelligent Systems: AI in Healthcare and a regular reviewer for numerous renowned journals. He has been involved in more than 100 academic, social, and market research projects. His research works are gaining interest in the scholarly world and have received increasing citations with an H-index of 25 and i-41 index (google scholars).
